01 Company Overview

Siemens India Limited (recently rebranded from Siemens Limited following the energy business demerger) is a subsidiary of Siemens AG, Germany and has been listed on Indian exchanges since 1957. The company operates at the intersection of electrification, automation, and digitalization, serving India's most critical infrastructure needs across energy systems, manufacturing, mobility, and smart buildings.

Headquartered in Mumbai, Siemens India employs over 11,000 professionals across manufacturing facilities at Kalwa, Nashik, Aurangabad, Goa, Gurgaon, and Vadodara. Its business spans four segments:

  • Energy: Transformers, switchgear, grid solutions, power automation (~30% pre-demerger)
  • Smart Infrastructure: Building technologies, low-voltage products, distribution systems (~25%)
  • Digital Industries: Factory automation, drives, PLCs, industrial software (~22%)
  • Mobility: Rail electrification, rolling stock, traffic management (~23%)
⚠ TWO CRITICAL STRUCTURAL CHANGES — Impact on Financials

1. Energy Business Demerger (effective 1 March 2025): Siemens India's large Energy business was spun off into a separately listed entity, Siemens Energy India. This significantly reduces the revenue base — LTM revenue falls from ₹19,524 Cr (FY24) to ₹17,337 Cr (post-demerger). All forward projections in this report are on a post-demerger basis.

2. Fiscal Year Change (from FY26 onwards): The company has changed its fiscal year-end from 30 September to 31 March. FY25 = Oct 2024–Sep 2025 (last old FY). FY26 onwards = April–March (new FY). Historical data (FY23A, FY24A) uses the Oct–Sep calendar.

📊 Trend Analysis

The stock has corrected sharply — down ~39% from its 52-week high of ₹5,520 — yet at ₹3,341.60 still trades at 68.9x LTM earnings, well above the capital goods sector median of ~35–40x. The Energy demerger has reduced the revenue base to ₹17,337 Cr, and while it improves the quality/margin profile of the remaining business, it also reduces absolute earnings for a transitional period. The DCF model implies the market-implied assumptions require ~17% revenue CAGR and ~40x+ EV/EBITDA on FY30 earnings — a very high bar.

03 Qualitative Analysis
a) Business Model

Core Products & Services (Post-Demerger): With the Energy segment spun off to Siemens Energy India, the remaining business is more concentrated in higher-margin, digital-intensive segments. The portfolio spans factory automation (PLCs, drives, robotics), building technologies, rail traction systems, and smart grid solutions. Services (AMCs, digital upgrades) are the fastest-growing stream at 12–15% of revenues, improving earnings quality and reducing cyclicality.

  • Projects & Long-Cycle Orders: Rail electrification, grid projects (~50–55%) — order-backed revenue visibility with ₹26,200 Cr loco order as marquee contract
  • Products & Solutions: Standardized equipment with shorter delivery cycles (~30–35%)
  • Services & Software: Annual maintenance, IoT platforms, digital upgrades (~12–15%)

Competitive Advantages: Global Siemens AG technology access, cross-segment bundling, 130+ year India brand equity, and localised manufacturing qualifying for government procurement — together form a wide and durable competitive moat that pure domestic players cannot replicate.

b) Management Quality

Siemens India is led by Sunil Mathur (MD & CEO), a long-tenured Siemens veteran with deep domain expertise. Governance standards are aligned with Siemens AG's global compliance framework. With Siemens AG holding 75% (at the maximum permitted limit), governance accountability is strengthened by the global parent's fiduciary oversight, though minority representation is structurally limited.

c) Growth Strategy

Key strategic pillars driving forward growth: (1) Railway electrification and metro rail expansion — over 1,000 km of metro lines under construction nationwide; (2) PLI-driven manufacturing upgrade demand for Digital Industries; (3) India's data centre boom requiring smart power infrastructure; (4) Ongoing margin improvement through mix shift to software and services within the Digital Industries segment. R&D from the Pune Technology Centre contributes to Siemens AG's global AI and edge computing roadmap, sustaining product relevance.

05 Investment Thesis
Key Drivers for Future Growth
  • India's Infrastructure Supercycle: Government capex at generational highs, with Siemens India as a diversified beneficiary across rail traction, grid, and smart buildings
  • Manufacturing Renaissance (PLI + China+1): Drives significant demand for Digital Industries — factory automation, PLCs, industrial drives
  • Rail Mega-Order (₹26,200 Cr Loco Contract): A multi-year revenue visibility anchor; execution risk is the primary swing factor per the DCF risk matrix
  • Services & Software Scaling: As the installed base compounds, high-margin service revenues grow organically, improving overall EBITDA margin profile toward 13% by FY30E
  • Data Economy Infrastructure: India's data centre boom (~3x capacity growth by 2027) creates demand for Smart Infrastructure segment solutions
Potential Catalysts — Upside & Downside
  • (Upside) Mega-Order Wins: HSR (High-Speed Rail) or DFCC Phase 2 wins could add ₹500–₹800/share to intrinsic value per the DCF risk matrix
  • (Upside) Margin Beat: If EBITDA margins reach 14% vs. modelled 13% by FY30E, the bull case DCF approaches ₹3,540 (Exit Multiple) — the only scenario with marginal upside from current prices
  • (Downside) Loco Order Execution Delays: Rated as HIGH probability risk by the model, with –₹500 to –₹800/share impact
  • (Downside) India Capex Slowdown: Medium probability; –₹400 to –₹600/share impact
  • (Downside) Rate Hike / WACC Rise: +100bps in WACC → ~₹150–₹250/share negative DCF impact
🎯 What Must Go Right to Justify ₹3,342

Per the DCF Verdict sheet, justifying the current price requires: ~17% revenue CAGR (vs. base case 12.7%), exit EV/EBITDA of ~40x+ (vs. global avg 14–18x), and flawless ₹26,200 Cr loco order delivery. This is a high-conviction bull scenario — possible, but not the base case. Even in the bull case, most DCF methods imply either marginal upside or moderate downside.

07 Conclusion

Siemens India Limited is an exceptional business in a structurally attractive market — but at ₹3,341.60, the stock is trading at valuations that price in a near-perfect future. The DCF analysis by Yatharth Bhatt is clear-eyed and rigorous: the base case fair value of ₹2,340 implies ~30% downside, and even the bull case (Exit Multiple, Rev CAGR 17%) yields only ₹3,540 — just +6% upside from current levels.

The qualitative story remains compelling: ROCE of 27.4%, a debt-free balance sheet with ₹4,500 Cr net cash, post-demerger clarity of business focus, and India's multi-decade infrastructure investment supercycle as a structural tailwind. The ₹26,200 Cr loco order is a significant revenue anchor, but the DCF risk matrix rates execution risk on this as HIGH probability.

The two structural changes — Energy demerger and fiscal year transition to April–March — create a period of earnings base adjustment and investor recalibration. The stock's 39% correction from ₹5,520 to ₹3,342 has improved the fundamental case meaningfully, but not enough to warrant fresh buying. The accumulation zone of ₹2,400–₹2,600 would offer a more attractive risk-reward, pricing in the base DCF with some margin of safety, and bringing the exit multiple bull case to 30%+ upside.

⚖ Final Verdict: HOLD / REDUCE — Target Entry: ₹2,400–₹2,600 | DCF Fair Value: ₹2,340 (Base) to ₹3,540 (Bull)

Siemens India warrants a position in a long-term industrial portfolio, but not at current prices. Existing holders should reduce on rallies toward ₹3,500+. New investors should await a correction toward the DCF base fair value range of ₹2,340–₹2,784, where the risk-reward improves materially. The business quality is not in question — the valuation is.
